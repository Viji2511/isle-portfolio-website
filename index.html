About Me

Academic & Clinical Biography

Prof. Balamurugan N. Appakalai, PhD, is an internationally acclaimed diabetes scientist who has spent over 25 years developing clinical-grade islet isolation technologies.

Currently serving as a Professor of Pediatrics at Nationwide Children's Hospital and The Ohio State University College of Medicine, Dr. Bala conducts research focused on developing novel islets and islet cell isolation methodologies for cellular transplantation in type 1 diabetes.

He previously held significant research and administrative roles at the University of Minnesota, the Wendy Novak Diabetes Institute (Norton Children's Hospital), and the University of Louisville, leading labs responsible for extracting high-yield, functional islets. His expertise spans human clinical allotransplantation, autotransplantation (TPIAT), and preclinical models in pigs and primates.

Dr. Bala's work has been published in elite journals like the American Journal of Transplantation and Transplantation, and he is a sought-after speaker globally, sharing standard operating procedures that shape islet processing laboratories around the world.

What is an Isletologist?

An Isletologist is a specialist in the science of pancreatic islets — the clusters of cells responsible for insulin production. Dr. Appakalai coined this term to describe a focused discipline that bridges islet biology, surgical procurement, cell processing, and clinical transplantation. This page shares his practical knowledge to help institutions worldwide build and advance their own islet programs.

Human Islet Isolation for Auto-Transplantation

Total pancreatectomy with islet auto-transplantation (TPIAT) offers relief from chronic pancreatitis while preserving insulin production. This expertise covers isolation from the patient's own pancreas for immediate re-infusion.

Scientific Domains

Research Focus & Discovery

Islet Isolation & Transplantation

Innovating clinical processing standards. We focus on enhancing islet yields, ensuring viability, and optimizing enzymatic digestive processes during human and porcine pancreas isolations.

  • Pancreatic Islets
  • Enzymatic Digestion
  • Allograft & Xenograft

Beta-Cell Regeneration

Exploring mechanisms that stimulate endogenous beta-cell proliferation. Investigating cell signaling pathways, peptide therapies, and chemical factors that trigger differentiation in progenitor lineages.

  • Stem Cells
  • Beta-Cell Biology
  • Endogenous Proliferation

Immunomodulation

Developing pathways to induce transplant tolerance. Creating strategies for micro-encapsulation and targeted immunotherapies that shield grafts from autoimmune recurrence and host rejection.

  • Micro-encapsulation
  • Immune Tolerance
  • Graft Protection

Pediatric Diabetes Research

Applying islet biology advancements to translational medicine for pediatric cohorts. Striving to design personalized cellular therapies that free children from intensive insulin dependencies.

  • Type 1 Diabetes
  • Pediatric Cohorts
  • Bedside Translation
